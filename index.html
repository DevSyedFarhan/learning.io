Alphabets are sets of letters used to represent sounds in a language. They form the basis for writing and communication. Different languages use different alphabets, like the 26-letter Latin alphabet used in English, or the Arabic and Cyrillic alphabets for other languages. Each letter in an alphabet has its own sound and contributes to forming words and sentences.
